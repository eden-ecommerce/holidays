# UK Christian Holiday Market Research: Extension & Gap Analysis

Following a comprehensive gap analysis of the top search results for "Christian holidays", several significant market segments and providers were identified that were not covered in the initial research phases. This extension document details these new findings, expanding the scope of the Christian holidays website to include niche markets, specialist activities, overseas tours, and lifestyle-specific retreats.

## 1. Niche & Specialist Pastoral Holidays

The initial research focused heavily on mainstream holidays and youth camps. However, there is a substantial and highly specific market for holidays that cater to pastoral needs, accessibility, and life stages.

### Accessible and Supported Holidays
Organisations exist specifically to ensure that Christians with disabilities can enjoy holidays with fellowship and appropriate support. **Through the Roof** runs "Together on Retreat" events and holidays specifically designed for disabled Christians, providing accessible accommodation and fellowship [1]. Similarly, **Torch Trust** runs "Torch Together Holidays" specifically tailored for blind and partially sighted Christians, aiming to help them grow in faith within a supportive community [2]. **Prospects** (now part of Livability) has historically been a major provider of Christian support and holidays for adults with learning disabilities [3].

### Bereavement and Pastoral Care
There is a growing market for gentle, supportive retreats for those experiencing grief. The **Living with Loss** project, for example, runs small retreats (6-14 participants) designed specifically for Christians struggling with bereavement [4]. Many retreat centres, such as the Archdiocese of Southwark, run specific "Grief in Loss, Hope in Christ" weekends away from the pressures of daily life [5].

### Singles and Seniors
While some mainstream operators cater to these groups, specific directories and focus areas exist. **Single Friendly Church** highlights that holidays can be a sore spot for single Christians and recommends specific companies like Oak Hall and Richmond Holidays that are welcoming to solo travellers without charging exorbitant single supplements [6]. For seniors, organisations like **Christian Endeavour Holiday Centres (CEHC)** operate Holiday Support Funds to provide assisted holidays for elderly guests or those with particular needs [7].

## 2. Overseas and International Tours

The initial research covered some UK-based overseas operators, but the gap analysis revealed several major international players and specific overseas holiday types that are highly popular with the UK market.

### Major Faith-Based Tour Operators
While McCabe and Tangney Tours are well-known UK brands, international operators have a significant presence. **Globus** offers extensive "Faith-Based & Religious Escorted Tours," taking pilgrims to Israel, Jordan, Egypt, and Europe [8]. **Trafalgar** also runs dedicated Christian tours, such as the "Martin Luther Reformation" tour in Germany and the "Days of Devotion" tour covering Marian shrines in France, Spain, and Portugal [9]. **Lightline Pilgrimages**, a UK-based operator, is a major player not previously covered, specialising in the Holy Land and explicitly focusing on supporting the "Living Stones" (local Christian communities) by using Christian-owned hotels and services [10].

### The Camino and European Pilgrimage
The **Camino de Santiago** is a massive market. Numerous operators, such as **UTracks**, **CaminoWays**, and **Andaspain**, offer fully guided or self-guided walking tours of the Camino specifically marketed to the UK [11]. The upcoming **Oberammergau Passion Play** in 2030 (performed every ten years) is another major driver of Christian tourism, with operators like **Titan Travel** and **Greatdays** offering dedicated packages [12].

### Christian Cruises
Faith-based cruising is a distinct sub-sector. Companies like **Inspiration Cruises** offer Christian Mediterranean cruises that trace the missionary journeys of the Apostle Paul, as well as dedicated Holy Land cruises [13].

## 3. Activity, Sport, and Creative Holidays

Beyond general activity camps, there are highly specialised holidays built around specific hobbies and creative pursuits, combined with Christian fellowship.

### Creative Arts and Writing
For those seeking artistic expression alongside spiritual growth, **Art and Faith Creative Retreats** offers international trips for women to explore creativity and faith [14]. In the UK, the **Creative Arts Retreat Movement (CARM)** and centres like **Quiet Waters** host specific art and journaling retreats [15]. There is also a distinct market for Christian writing retreats, such as those hosted by the **Union School of Theology** and **Scargill House**, aimed at authors wanting to craft books with theological depth [16].

### Specialist Sports
Christian sports holidays are surprisingly diverse. **Christian Surfers UK** organises national gatherings, camp-outs, and events in Cornwall, combining surfing with worship [17]. For cyclists, the **Christian Fellowship of Cyclists** and operators like **Christian Adventures** offer dedicated road cycling trips [18]. There are even dedicated Christian golf retreats and fellowships [19].

## 4. Alternative Accommodation: Camping, Canals, and Champing

The accommodation landscape for Christian holidays extends far beyond traditional hotels and retreat centres.

### Champing (Church Camping)
A unique and growing trend in the UK is **Champing™**, operated by The Churches Conservation Trust. This allows guests to camp overnight in historic, often redundant, churches. It provides exclusive use of ancient sacred spaces and helps fund the preservation of the buildings [20].

### Boating and Caravanning Fellowships
The **Boaters’ Christian Fellowship** caters to Christians who love the UK's inland waterways, organising get-togethers and canal boat holidays [21]. Similarly, the **Caravanners and Campers Christian Fellowship (CCCF)** organises rallies across the UK where believers meet in tents and caravans for fellowship, morning prayers, and worship [22].

### Christian Holiday Parks
For families seeking a traditional holiday park experience with a Christian ethos, **Le Pas Opton** in France (run by Spring Harvest Holidays) offers a 4-star park with optional Bible study and worship [23]. In the UK, sites like **Cefn Lea** in Wales provide large-scale Christian conference and holiday park facilities [24].

## 5. Voluntourism and Mission Holidays

A significant segment of the Christian travel market involves short-term mission trips and voluntourism, combining travel with service.

### Short-Term Missions
Organisations like **Operation Mobilisation (OM)** and **Youth With A Mission (YWAM)** offer short-term mission trips ranging from one week to several months, allowing UK Christians to serve overseas [25]. **Mission Direct** specialises in two-week overseas mission trips for UK volunteers to build homes or schools in developing nations [26].

### Charity Supporter Trips
Major charities also run specific travel experiences. **Compassion International** runs "Field Experiences," allowing sponsors to travel in groups to visit the children they sponsor and see the charity's work firsthand [27]. **Tearfund** runs "Discovery Team" trips designed to help church leaders and donors understand their humanitarian work [28].
